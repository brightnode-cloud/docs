Follow this guide to learn how to create an account, deploy your first GPU Node, and use it to execute code remotely.

## Step 1: Create an account

Start by creating a Brightnode account:

1. [Sign up here](https://www.console.brightnode.cloud/signup).
2. Verify your email address.
3. Set up two-factor authentication (recommended for security).

<Tip>

Planning to share compute resources with your team? You can convert your personal account to a team account later. See [Manage accounts](/get-started/manage-accounts) for details.

</Tip>

## Step 2: Deploy a Node

Now that you've created your account, you're ready to deploy your first Node:

1. Open the [Nodes page](https://www.console.brightnode.cloud/nodes) in the web interface.
2. Click the **Deploy** button.
3. Select **A40** from the list of graphics cards.
4. In the field under **Node Name**, enter the name **quickstart-node**.
5. Keep all other fields (Node Template, GPU Count, and Instance Pricing) on their default settings.
6. Click **Deploy On-Demand** to deploy and start your Node. You'll be redirected back to the Nodes page after a few seconds.

<Note>

If you haven't set up payments yet, you'll be prompted to add a payment method and purchase credits for your account.

</Note>

## Step 3: Explore the Node detail pane

On the [Nodes page](https://www.console.brightnode.cloud/nodes), click the Node you just created to open the Node detail pane. The pane opens onto the **Connect** tab, where you'll find options for connecting to your Node so you can execute code on your GPU (after it's done initializing).

Take a minute to explore the other tabs: 

- **Details**: Information about your Node, such as hardware specs, pricing, and storage.
- **Telemetry**: Realtime utilization metrics for your Node's CPU, memory, and storage.
- **Logs**: Logs streamed from your container (including stdout from any applications inside) and the Node management system.
- **Template Readme**: Details about the template your Node is running. Your Node is configured with the latest official Brightnode PyTorch template.

## Step 4: Execute code on your Node with JupyterLab

1. Go back to the **Connect** tab, and under **HTTP Services**, click **Jupyter Lab** to open a JupyterLab workspace on your Node.
2. Under **Notebook**, select **Python 3 (ipykernel)**.
3. Type `print("Hello, world!")` in the first line of the notebook.
4. Click the play button to run your code.

## Step 5: Clean up

To avoid incurring unnecessary charges, follow these steps to clean up your Node resources:

1. Return to the [Nodes page](https://www.console.brightnode.cloud/nodes) and click your running Node.
2. Click the **Stop** button (pause icon) to stop your Node.
3. Click **Stop Node** in the modal that opens to confirm.

You'll still be charged a small amount for storage on stopped Nodes (\$0.20 per GB per month). If you don't need to retain any data on your Node, you should terminate it completely.

To terminate your Node:

1. Click the **Terminate** button (trash icon).
2. Click **Terminate Node** to confirm.

<Warning>

Terminating a Node permanently deletes all data that isn't stored in a [network volume](/storage/network-volumes). Be sure that you've saved any data you might need to access again.

To learn more about how storage works, see the [Node storage overview](/nodes/storage/types).

</Warning>
